    • Cumilla:The city of pilgrims

           Cumilla, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is a city steeped in history and culture, known as "Kamala" in ancient Sanskrit texts and boasting influences from Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic traditions. Its economy thrives on agriculture, particularly rice and jute production, and it serves as an educational hub with institutions like Cumilla University.

      Tourists are drawn to Cumilla's historical and religious sites, including the ancient Buddhist monastery Shalban Vihara and the Mainamati War Cemetery.

      This city's strategic location ensures robust transportation links, facilitating trade and travel connections to other major cities, including the capital, Dhaka.

      In summary, Cumilla is an ancient city in Bangladesh, offering a rich historical and cultural tapestry, economic vitality, educational excellence, and alluring tourist attractions.

  • Food

    • Roshmolai

      •      Matri Bandar Roshmolai is a well-known sweet shop located in Cumilla, Bangladesh, famous for its delicious and authentic Roshmolai, among other Bengali sweets. This sweet shop has gained a reputation for producing high-quality sweets that cater to the taste buds of locals and visitors alike.

      Matri Bandar Roshmolai offers a variety of sweets, but their Roshmolai is a standout delicacy. It follows the traditional recipe, featuring soft paneer dumplings soaked in creamy milk syrup, garnished with saffron strands and chopped nuts. The combination of rich, creamy milk and the delicate texture of the dumplings makes their Roshmolai a popular choice for those with a sweet tooth.

      Visitors to Cumilla often seek out Matri Bandar Roshmolai to savor the authentic flavors of this beloved Bengali dessert. It's not just a sweet treat but also a cultural experience, as Bengali sweets like Roshmolai are an integral part of celebrations and gatherings in the region. If you're in Cumilla and have a craving for Roshmolai, Matri Bandar is a place where you can indulge in this delightful dessert.

    • Misti

While the specific details of this sweet may vary among different sweet shops and households, it typically consists of a combination of ingredients like milk, sugar, ghee (clarified butter), and sometimes flour or semolina. These ingredients are cooked together to create a rich and creamy dessert.

  • Cloth

    • Khadi

      •     Khadi cloth, celebrated for its handwoven, natural fiber qualities, embodies a cherished tradition in Bangladesh, including the region of Cumilla. Crafted from cotton, Khadi's reputation for breathability and comfort suits the warm, humid climate, and its artisanal, handwoven nature imparts uniqueness and historical significance to each piece.

      Skilled artisans in Cumilla employ traditional techniques, involving spinning cotton into yarn, natural dyeing, and weaving on wooden looms. This labor-intensive process yields eco-friendly textiles that champion local craftsmanship.

      Khadi from Cumilla enjoys widespread acclaim for its quality, adorning a range of clothing items like saris, kurtas, and shirts, symbolizing a deep connection to the region's heritage and culture. With historical importance dating back to the struggle for independence in 1971, Khadi remains an emblem of self-reliance, sustainability, and cultural identity in Bangladesh.

      Khadi products are readily found in local markets, artisan workshops, and Khadi outlets throughout Cumilla, attracting tourists and conscientious shoppers alike, eager to embrace and support this enduring tradition while appreciating its cultural and environmental significance.
